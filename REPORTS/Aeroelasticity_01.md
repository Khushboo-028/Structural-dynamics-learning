# Aeroelasticity — Conceptual Foundation

## 1. Why does aeroelasticity exist?
## Ans. When a body is subjected to incoming flow , it exerts aerodynamic forces (fluid forcing) on the body , and the elastic restoring forces prevents the structural movement , if the system is dynamic the inertial forces come into picture. The interaction of these 3 types of forces on the body subjected to a airstream, causes the existence of aeroelasticity. 
 
## 2. What are the three interacting disciplines in the aeroelastic triangle?
## Ans. Fluid Mechanics(Aerodynamic forcing), Solid Mechanics(Elastic restoring forces), Structural dynamics(inertial forces). 
## 3. How is an aeroelastic problem different from a normal structural dynamics problem?
## Ans. In aeroelasticity, there is continuous change of angle of attack and interaction of fluid forcing with the structure, which cannot be accommodated in the normal structural dynamics problem. 

## 4. What is static aeroelastic instability?
## Ans. In static aeroelastic instability, when the aerodynamic effects overgrows the elastic effects, when inertial effects are very less, which is called as divergence.

## 5. What is dynamic aeroelastic instability?
## Ans. When the bluff body is not stationary and has significant inertial effects, the instability is dynamic.

## 6. Why is flutter called a self-excited instability?
## Ans. Flutter is caused by the interaction of structural and bending modes, when the structure moves, the further addition of the energy excites the structure more and the loop continues to grow, hence it is called self excited instability.

## 7. Why is galloping generally low frequency?
## Ans. The galloping structure is mounted on an elastic body the stiffness is high, so the excitation occurs at the natural frequency of the structure causing the lower frequency vibration.

## 8. What is the physical difference between flutter and galloping?
## Ans. The flutter is due to interaction of two modes (modal interaction) whereas galloping is due to the assymetric shear layers around a bluff body(non-cylindrical). Flutter is a high frequency self excited oscillation and galloping is a low frequency oscillation
